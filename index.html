Select a device and learn the correct field method.

Level

Radar level transmitter

For Vega VEGAPULS, Endress+Hauser Micropilot, Siemens Sitrans LR and similar radar instruments.

  1. Confirm nozzle position, range, process temperature and dielectric.
  2. Wire loop power, screen the cable and check polarity before energizing.
  3. Set empty and full distances, damping, output mode and echo suppression.
Flow

Flow measurement

Magnetic, Coriolis, vortex and DP flow systems from leading automation vendors.

  1. Verify pipe orientation, straight runs, grounding rings and process fill.
  2. Connect power, signal, pulse and communication terminals as per datasheet.
  3. Zero the meter, set engineering units, span and totalizer behavior.
Pressure

Pressure and DP transmitter

Gauge, absolute and differential pressure transmitters for tanks, lines and filters.

  1. Mount above or below tapping points according to gas, liquid or steam service.
  2. Check impulse tubing, manifolds, vents, drains and isolation valves.
  3. Apply five-point calibration and document as-found and as-left results.
Temperature

Temperature loop

RTD, thermocouple and head-mount transmitter installation for process temperature.

  1. Inspect thermowell insertion length, lagging and vibration risk.
  2. Wire 2, 3 or 4 wire RTD correctly and keep thermocouple polarity intact.
  3. Simulate sensor input and trim transmitter output to the control system.
VSD

Variable speed drive

Install, wire and commission VSDs/VFDs for pumps, fans, conveyors and mixers.

  1. Confirm supply, motor nameplate, enclosure rating, cooling space and earthing.
  2. Separate power and control wiring, fit EMC glands and connect STO if required.
  3. Enter motor data, acceleration, deceleration, control source and protection limits.
Analytical

pH, ORP, conductivity and analyzers

Water, chemical, boiler, effluent and process analyzers with local transmitter and probe wiring.

  1. Install probe where flow is representative, bubbles are avoided and the sensor stays wet.
  2. Use shielded low-noise cable, separate sensor cable from power, and keep junction boxes dry.
  3. Calibrate with fresh certified buffers or standards, rinse between points and record temperature.
Control

Control valves and positioners

Pneumatic, electro-pneumatic and smart positioners used on globe, ball, butterfly and damper actuators.

  1. Check air supply quality, tubing leaks, actuator spring range and mechanical linkage travel.
  2. Wire 4-20 mA command, feedback, limit switches and solenoids to the correct PLC or DCS channels.
  3. Run auto-stroke, verify fail action, set split range if used and confirm valve travel at 0, 25, 50, 75 and 100 percent.
Safety

Gas, flame and fire detection

LEL, toxic gas, oxygen deficiency, flame scanners, smoke and heat detectors for plant protection systems.

  1. Mount at the correct height for the gas density and keep detectors clear of water, dust and dead-air zones.
  2. Wire power, signal and alarm contacts through approved barriers or isolators where required.
  3. Perform bump test and calibration with certified gas; verify PLC alarms, voting logic and trip action.
Mechanical

Vibration, speed and weighing

Accelerometers, proximity probes, tachometers, encoders, load cells, belt scales and weigh feeders.

  1. Mount sensors on clean, rigid surfaces and route cables away from VSD output and high-current conductors.
  2. Wire IEPE, proximity driver, pulse, encoder or mV/V load cell signals into the correct module type.
  3. Calibrate zero/span with known mass, speed reference, shaker table or vendor simulator as applicable.
Switches

Level, pressure and temperature switches

Point-level, pressure, flow and temperature switches used for alarms, permissives and protection.

  1. Install where the process actually reaches the switch and where vibration or turbulence will not chatter the contact.
  2. Wire dry contacts to digital input common correctly; use interposing relays for different voltages.
  3. Prove switch point and reset point with a controlled process, hand pump, bath or simulator.
Wiring

Loop checking and handover

Trace every circuit from field device to junction box, marshalling cabinet, PLC or DCS input.

  1. Megger cables only when devices are disconnected and site rules allow it.
  2. Inject 4, 12 and 20 mA and confirm correct tag, scaling and alarm direction.
  3. Record cable number, terminal number, range, units and final status.

PLC wiring

How field instruments connect to PLC input and output cards.

Terminal names differ between PLC brands, but the principle is the same: match the signal type to the correct module, maintain polarity, keep shields controlled and prove the value at the PLC before handover.

+24 V / loop positive Analog signal Command / feedback 0 V / common / return Cable shield to earth at one end

2-wire 4-20 mA transmitter to analog input

PSU + to TX + TX - to PLC AI I+ PLC AI COM to PSU - Screen to panel earth only
  • Use analog input set to current mode, not voltage mode.
  • Connect shield to instrument earth or panel earth at one end only unless the site standard says otherwise.
  • PLC scaling example: 4 mA = 0 percent, 12 mA = 50 percent, 20 mA = 100 percent.

4-wire transmitter to analog input

Supply L+ to TX P+ Supply M to TX P- TX AO+ to PLC I+ TX AO- to PLC COM
  • Power and signal are separate circuits; do not place the PLC input in the supply line.
  • Check whether the output is active or passive before wiring.
  • For voltage output, use PLC voltage input and scale the engineering range correctly.

Digital sensor or switch to PLC input

+24 V through field contact Contact return to PLC IN PLC COM to 0 V Use relay if voltages differ
  • Sourcing input usually receives +24 V through the field contact; sinking input wiring may be opposite.
  • Use interposing relay contacts when field voltage differs from PLC input voltage.
  • Prove open and closed state in the PLC tag monitor and check alarm inversion.

PLC analog output to valve positioner or VSD speed

PLC AO+ to device + Device - to PLC AO COM Feedback to separate AI if used Confirm active/passive loop
  • Confirm active/passive loop requirements for the AO card and receiving device.
  • Scale command as 4 mA = closed/minimum and 20 mA = open/maximum unless the process requires reverse action.
  • Stroke the final element and verify feedback, travel limits, fail position and interlocks.

RTD and thermocouple to PLC temperature module

RTD A to module A RTD B to module B RTD b to module b TC polarity must match cable type
  • Use a temperature input module, not a standard 4-20 mA card, unless a transmitter is installed.
  • RTD 3-wire: two same-color leads usually go to B and b; single lead goes to A.
  • Thermocouple: maintain polarity and use the correct extension cable type to the cold junction module.

Pulse, frequency and encoder signals

A phase to HSC A B phase to HSC B Z index to HSC Z Shield earth at PLC end
  • High-speed signals need a high-speed counter or motion module, not a slow digital input.
  • Set pulses per revolution, engineering units, debounce/filter and direction logic.
  • Compare displayed speed, count or total with a handheld tachometer or known reference.

Calibration workflow

From bench setup to signed handover.

4-20 mA transmitter method

Connect a loop calibrator in series. Record as-found at 0, 25, 50, 75 and 100 percent. For a 0-10 bar range, apply 0, 2.5, 5, 7.5 and 10 bar and expect 4, 8, 12, 16 and 20 mA. Adjust only if outside tolerance, then repeat upscale and downscale.

Pressure and DP method

Isolate and vent safely. Connect a certified pressure source to the correct side. Equalize DP before zero. Apply five points slowly, allow stabilization, tap lightly only if procedure permits, then check hysteresis on the return points.

Temperature method

For transmitter calibration, simulate RTD resistance or thermocouple mV with cold-junction compensation enabled. For sensor calibration, use a dry block or bath with a reference probe and allow enough soak time before recording readings.

Flow method

For magmeters and Coriolis meters, perform zero only with a full pipe and no flow. For DP flow, calibrate the DP transmitter to the calculated range and verify square-root extraction in transmitter or PLC, but not both.

Level method

For radar or ultrasonic, measure empty distance, full distance, nozzle height and blocking distance. Confirm echo quality, map false echoes, then test output at known level or by simulation. For DP level, calculate wet-leg or dry-leg range before applying pressure.

Analytical method

Clean probe, inspect electrolyte or membrane, then calibrate with fresh standards. pH normally uses pH 7 first, then pH 4 or 10. Conductivity uses a certified standard near the process value. Record sample temperature and slope/offset.

Control valve method

Confirm air supply, fail action and mechanical travel. Command 4, 8, 12, 16 and 20 mA. Verify actual stem or shaft position, feedback signal, travel limits, seat leakage requirement and response without hunting.

VSD commissioning method

Enter motor nameplate voltage, current, frequency, speed and power. Run motor identification if allowed. Verify direction uncoupled where possible, then test 0, 25, 50, 75 and 100 percent speed reference, start/stop source, fault relay and emergency stop chain.

01

Identify

Check tag, service, hazardous area rating, range, units, process connection and datasheet revision.

02

Isolate

Follow permit, LOTO and bypass procedure. Protect the plant before disconnecting or simulating.

03

Simulate

Apply known pressure, temperature, level or electrical signal at 0, 25, 50, 75 and 100 percent.

04

Trim

Adjust sensor trim or output trim only when error exceeds tolerance and the procedure allows it.

05

Document

Record as-found, as-left, serial number, calibrator ID, technician, date and next due date.
